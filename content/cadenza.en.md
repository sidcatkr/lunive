## Discord music with a real interface

Most Discord music bots ask you to live inside a chat box. Cadenza does not. You start a session with a single slash command, and the bot hands you back a private dashboard URL. Open it on any browser and you have a full music control surface — search, queue, lyrics, playback — all visible at once, no commands required.

## What's actually there

**One slash command, zero setup.** Type `/play` in Discord, and Cadenza joins your voice channel and gives you a private dashboard link. No roles to configure. No prefix to remember.

**Click any lyric to seek there.** Synced lyrics scroll alongside the song, and every line is interactive — tap one to jump playback to that moment.

**Album art sets the mood.** The dashboard background tints to whatever is currently playing, so the room feels like the song instead of a control panel for the song.

**Search across multiple sources.** Type a song name or paste a URL. Results appear instantly across YouTube and SoundCloud — pick whichever sounds best.

**Audio effects are built in.** A 5-band EQ and presets like Bass Boost, Nightcore, and Vaporwave are available right in the dashboard.

**Your favorites and playlists follow you.** Sign in with Discord and your liked songs and playlists are persistent across servers and devices.

**Premium polish, free to use.** Cadenza is open and free. No paywalls in the way of the music.

## Why it exists

Cadenza is the first product from [Lunive](/en/about). It started from a simple frustration: managing music through chat commands is a workaround, not an experience. The slash commands are how you start a session; the dashboard is where you actually live. Read more about that thinking in [Building Cadenza](/en/stories/building-cadenza).
